First Days on the water

Zone out ,end up in the soup

Starting Off , rowers oftern begin in the tub. A large high boyancey wooden boat is used for the individual to learn how to balance the boat, this "training wheel" period is sometimes done in small pools on land where the boat sits in a larger tub filled with water. The next stage is the practice launch in which the boat is launched parallel to the shor for river rowing and perpendicular for sea rowing. The initiate climbs aboard placing emphesis on the location of oar handles abpve all other factors as this the most important factor if one whishes to stay dry.

Seen below are several images of the different boat types begginers often start in .There is

    The Tub ,a large midsection for stability and drag [optimum for sea and river ] , used for balance and "starts" practice.

    The Scull ,a slim highly manouverable single class ,used for balanceing stroke rate and power ,[river rowing ONLY], used for constistent stroke attribute training

    The Double , just a larger version of standard scull, usually comes in two standards , with or without coxen [driver], used for rythm, team and technique training
